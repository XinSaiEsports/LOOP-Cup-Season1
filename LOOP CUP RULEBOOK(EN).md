## 1. Schedule & Format
 
-  1.1 Tournament Format
 
    -   1.1.1 Tournament Rules: This championship does not offer direct invitations based on VRS List; all participants will be invited on a "first-come, first-served" basis through open registration.
 
    -   1.1.2 The tournament consists of two stages: the Closed Qualifier and the Playoffs.

    -   1.1.3 Closed Qualifier: This stage will be held online. The game server will be hosted in China. 16 teams will be divided into two groups, A and B, using a double-elimination format. There will be 8 teams in each group. All matches will be BO3. The top two teams from each group will advance to the Playoffs.

    -   1.1.4 Playoffs: This stage will be held offline in Shanghai and will use a single-elimination format. The Final will be BO5, and all other matches will be BO3.
  
    -   1.1.5 The tournament will use the following seven maps: OVERPASS, MIRAGE, INFERNO, DUST2, NUKE, ANCIENT, ANUBIS.

    -   1.1.6 During the Closed Qualifier, there will be no coach slot on the server. Coaches may stand behind the players during the match.

-  1.2 Schedule

    -   Closed Qualifiers Dates: March 14  to 15, 2026 (Beijing Time)

    -   Playoffs Dates: March 21 to 22, 2026 (Beijing Time)

## 2. Anti cheating camera

-  2.1.1 During online matches, participants must abide by the principle of fair competition, follow the referee's arrangements, and cooperate with anti-cheating inspections related to the tournament.

-  2.1.2  When specifically stated,each player in an online event must be able to provide a clean camera feed.Every participant needs to activate their camera a minimum of thirty (30) minutes before the start time of the match.Participants may turn off their cameras between maps in a series but must turn them back on a minimum of five (5) minutes before the start of the next map.Failure to provide a clean camera feed for the full duration of the map (including pre-match) will result in the participant being subject to fines.If at any point during the map the camera feed is not provided,it will be considered missing for the entire mapand will also result in a fine.

-  2.1.3 If aparticipant fails to fulfil the camera obligations in more than two matches in the same competition,they will be considered a repeat offender and the standard punishments will be increased.

-  2.1.4 For online competitions,players may be required to provide at least two live camera feeds  showing their setup-including,but not limited to,their monitor,key board,mouse,and surrounding area.Specific requirements will be communicated on a case-by-casebasis. Compliance with these instructions is mandatory;failure to do so will result in penalties,up to  and including disqualification from the competition.
    
-  2.1.5 The camera must provide a clear view with a correct angle. The required shooting perspective is a 45-degree angle from the rear side of each participant. The recorded footage must include the participant's body, screen, mouse, and keyboard. If all five team members are in the same room, one camera/video recorder must be installed at the back; if each player is in a separate room, a camera/video recorder must be equipped behind each player.

-  2.1.6 Participants shall enter Tencent Meeting or access other websites as required by the event organizer and turn on their cameras.

-  2.1.7 All participants must record the first-person match footage and third-person footage (showing hands, face, and screen) of the match throughout the entire match day. The recordings must be uploaded to Baidu Netdisk and shared with the tournament organizer by 3:00 AM the next day. Only Baidu Netdisk is accepted, and the sharing period must be set to permanent.

## 3. Player Facial Camera Requirements

-  3.1.1 During the online match stage, players are required to use facial cameras/video recorders.

-  3.1.2 Facial perspective requirements: Frontal or side view with reasonable lighting conditions to clearly capture the player's facial expressions.

-  3.1.3 Participants shall enter Tencent Meeting or access other websites as required by the event organizer and turn on their cameras.

## 4. Equipment Requirements

-  4.1.1 Participants must bring their own peripherals, including mouse, keyboard, mouse pad, and in-ear headphones. The tournament organizer will provide over-ear headphones for sound insulation and microphone communication. The tournament is not responsible for replacing participants' self-provided peripherals in case of malfunctions.
